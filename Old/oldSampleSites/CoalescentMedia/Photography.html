Photography: The newest addition to the portfolio, and yet the earliest beginnings of my creative sojourn.

Harken back, if you will, to a simpler time. A time when the Apple Macintosh had not yet fully replaced its Apple II sibling, and the 'IBM Clone' advocates scoffed at the computer mouse and the Graphical User Interface. It was in those days that I began my photographic experience; learning to harness the power of German optics, damp, dark, red-tinged rooms, and Ilford Multigrade.

Having logged hundreds of hours shooting and developing untold thousands of images for campus publications, budding Thespians, and the terminally 'In Love', I traded in my light meter for a punch-card. My "hobby" was set aside for a "Real Job."

Much has changed since then. 'Mouse' and 'Windows' are both words now used in ways that would confound those living prior to the Reagan Presidency. Silver Halide has been supplanted by the Mega-Pixel CMOS sensor, the Brothers Knoll gifted the world with Photoshop, and the term "Real Job" has gained a whole new meaning.

We're back, Baby!
