DVD and Multimedia

If you are looking to distribute your message with the highest picture and sound quality, DVD is the answer. Add in such features as interactivity, closed captioning and multiple language and subtitle tracks, and you have a powerhouse format.

For those looking to reach the widest audience of computer users, look no further than our in-house design team. We have the tools and the talent to publish your vision, either on CD-ROM or on the Web.

VIDEOWORKSHOP can handle your DVD and Multimedia projects from design to replication. From a simple 'video on a disc' to a highly interactive title, we produce a professional product. The production process goes like this:

Design: For interactive titles, we can design menus and interoperability features so it works for you and your audience. We'll consult with you to make sure your media assets are prepared so we can then design and program them to work together.

Encoding: The video and audio materials for you project are encoded to the appropriate format. DVD programs are encoded into MPEG-2, while CD-ROM assets are encoded in your choice of Quicktime, MPEG, Real, and/or Windows Media.

Menu Creation: We can create still or motion menus to your specifications. If you prefer, we'll also provide you with the information necessary to submit your own menu art. We're flexible.

Authoring: In this step, we script the interface to function correctly for the end-user.

Disc Recording: Once encoded and authored, the disc is ready to be 'burned'. This can be used as either a prototype, or as the final product.

Replication: We can mass replicate your title by outputting to Digital Linear Tape (DLT), which is then sent to a replication facility where a master disc is cut. From this master, copies are professionally pressed and labeled.
